Tesla just released a balance bike for toddlers, and as all balance bikes do, this one has no pedals, no motor, no brakes - the legs do all the things. However, it has a nice, clean design and a lightweight magnesium frame. The catch? It's priced at $225.

It's designed for kids aged between 2 and 5 and has a five-way adjustable seat, but even if you and your kids like it, it's already out of stock, even at this price. Tesla even sold out a $30 matching T-shirt. We don't know how many were produced to begin with, though. The good news is Tesla is planning to sell another batch with pre-orders scheduled do begin on July 31, but you need to be quick.

Most balance bikes go for around $100, so Tesla charging $225 is a lot, and it refuses to make a proper e-bike for adults because Elon Musk thinks they are dangerous.

Tesla has produced kids' products in the past, like the Cyberquad ATV, which was paused for a while because it didn't comply with ATV safety standards.
