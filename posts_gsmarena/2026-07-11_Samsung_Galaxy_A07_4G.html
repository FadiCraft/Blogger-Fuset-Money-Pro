Introduction

The Galaxy A0-series is Samsung's most affordable entry point into the Galaxy ecosystem, and the newly launched Galaxy A07 4G is no exception. At roughly €70, it is positioned squarely in the ultra-budget segment, competing with the likes of Realme's C-series and Redmi's entry-level lineup.

Despite its modest price, Samsung has managed to pack in a few welcome upgrades compared to last year's A06 4G, most notably a smoother 90Hz refresh rate for its large 6.7-inch LCD. For this price bracket, that's a meaningful quality-of-life improvement, and one that helps the A07 stand out in its class.

The A07 4G also benefits from Samsung's new software support policy, promising up to six major Android upgrades - an unheard-of commitment at this price tier. This alone could be a major selling point for users looking for a phone that lasts beyond just a couple of years.

The 5000mAh battery with 25W fast charging support is another strength, ensuring solid endurance with relatively quick top-ups for the category.

Samsung Galaxy A07 4G specs at a glance:

  • Body: 167.4x77.4x7.6mm, 184g; Glass front, plastic back, plastic frame; IP54 dust protected and water resistant (water splashes).
  • Display: 6.70" PLS LCD, 90Hz, 720x1600px resolution, 20:9 aspect ratio, 262ppi.
  • Chipset: Mediatek Helio G99 (6 nm): Octa-core (2x2.2 GHz Cortex-A76 & 6x2.0 GHz Cortex-A55); Mali-G57 MC2.
  • Memory: 64GB 4GB RAM, 128GB 4GB RAM, 128GB 6GB RAM, 256GB 8GB RAM; UFS 2.2; microSDXC (dedicated slot).
  • OS/Software: Android 15, up to 6 major Android upgrades, One UI 7.
  • Rear camera: Wide (main): 50 MP, f/1.8, 1/2.76", 0.64µm, PDAF; Depth: 2 MP, f/2.4.
  • Front camera: 8 MP, f/2.0, (wide), 1/4.0", 1.12µm.
  • Video capture: Rear camera: 1080p@30/60fps; Front camera: Yes.
  • Battery: 5000mAh; 25W wired.
  • Connectivity: LTE; Wi-Fi 5; BT 5.3; FM radio; 3.5mm jack.
  • Misc: Fingerprint reader (side-mounted).

Samsung hasn't skimped entirely on performance either. The MediaTek Helio G99, although a few years old, remains one of the more capable chips in the entry-level space, offering memory configurations up to 8GB of RAM and 256GB of storage, along with a dedicated microSD slot for further expansion.

The 50MP main camera, flanked by a simple depth sensor, should deliver respectable daylight shots, though expectations need to remain tempered in low-light scenarios. The 8MP selfie camera also covers the basics.

Of course, corners had to be cut to hit the sub-€100 price. The display still sticks to HD+ resolution, and the panel is of the basic PLS LCD variety and not particularly bright. There is no NFC for contactless payments. The design is very utilitarian, with a plastic build, though Samsung does add IP54 splash resistance - a small but welcome touch in this category. Multimedia fans will appreciate the presence of a 3.5mm headphone jack, FM radio, and a loudspeaker, but stereo speakers are missing.

Overall, the Galaxy A07 4G is an incremental but meaningful step forward for Samsung's entry-level lineup. It's not without limitations, but the higher refresh rate, long software support, and solid endurance make it one of the most balanced phones you can get for around €70. Let's see how well it delivers in practice.

Unboxing

The Galaxy A07 4G comes in a very simple two-piece cardboard box. It is not particularly thick, which gives us pause when it comes to protecting the phone during transit. Still, it did the job well for our unit.

On the plus side, there is no plastic anywhere in the packaging, which is good from an ecological standpoint.

Samsung didn't splurge in the accessory department. The Galaxy A07 4G only ships with a basic USB Type-C to Type-C passive cable and nothing else. No charger, no case, nothing.
