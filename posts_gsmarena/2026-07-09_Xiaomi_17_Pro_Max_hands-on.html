Introduction

Xiaomi is skipping the number 16 for this year's flagships, and it's jumping straight to 17, implying that it's ready to take on the iPhone 17 series directly. Further underlining those ambitions, the Xiaomi 17 Pro and 17 Pro Max are very similar to the iPhone 17 counterparts design-wise, but there's a big twist.

Xiaomi's latest Pro models feature a secondary OLED screen right next to the cameras, which is undoubtedly their most eye-catching feature. It's not something we haven't seen before. The Xiaomi 11 Ultra from a few years ago had a second screen on its back, but the one here is bigger, brighter and overall much better.

The secondary display measures 2.9" and has the same 120 Hz refresh rate as the main one. Peak brightness on both is reportedly 3,500 nits, according to Xiaomi.

Before we move on, we have to make it clear that we obtained this review device from China as Xiaomi has not yet announced its plans for releasing it to other markets. When and if it does, the globally released product may get tweaks or specs differences, so we will review it separately again.

Moving on with the Xiaomi 17 Pro Max update overview, you get a new front screen as well. The Pro Max features a 6.9-inch LTPO OLED panel with thinner, symmetrical bezels all around. It's also said to be brighter than the previous lineup.

The 17 Pro Max is the first phone released with the latest Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5, promising performance and efficiency improvements, as always. Impressively, the 17 Pro Max comes with 512GB storage in its base version.

The camera system gets a small update, with the main camera now featuring a slightly larger sensor, the new Light Fusion 950L. The 50MP 5x telephoto did get minor tweaks to the prism layout, allowing a bigger 1/2" sensor as well. The 50MP ultrawide unit features autofocus, enabling it to capture close-ups. You can also take excellent close-up shots with the zoom camera as well, just don't expect telemacro level of magnification here - at 30cm, the minimum focusing distance is not so short.

Xiaomi is finally replacing the old 32MP selfie camera with a seemingly much better 50MP shooter with autofocus. A well overdue update.

The battery receives a massive update as well. The 17 Pro Max is now equipped with a power pack worthy of a small tablet, boasting 7,500 mAh capacity. There's no compromise in the fast charging either, as the handset supports 100W wired and 50W wireless charging. That's even a small step up from last year's Xiaomi 15 Pro.
