Google is already teasing the Pixel 11 series, which will be unveiled on August 12. These could be the first phones to feature a chipset from TSMC’s 2nm fab and are expected to swap the Samsung modem for a MediaTek one. We’ve even seen leaked prices for the US market.

Rumors suggest that the new Pro and Pro XL will change their base configuration to 12/256GB, losing 4GB of RAM. 16GB of RAM will be available only on the 512GB variants.

That was one of the best features on the Google Pixel 10 Pro – 16GB of RAM is rare on a US flagship. You won’t get it from Samsung (unless you find a 16GB/1TB S26 Ultra) and you won’t get it from Apple either.
