Introduction

The Infinix Note 60 lineup has turned out quite interesting this year, delivering unique looks, features and even packages. After reviewing the Pininfarina-designed Note 60 Ultra, it's time to explore the mid-range Infinix Note 60 Pro.

The Infinix Note 60 Pro shares some key features with the Ultra model, while exchanging a step back in camera performance for a more affordable price tag.

The model is advertised for its aluminum unibody design and enhanced cooling system. It features a 6.78-inch AMOLED screen with 1,208p resolution, 144Hz refresh rate, and HDR support. Gorilla Glass 7i handles display protection.

The Note 60 Pro is powered by the Snapdragon 7s Gen 4 chip coupled with up to 12GB RAM and up to 256GB storage. It is a notable step up from the Helio G100 inside the Note 50 Pro 4G.

The camera department is not as premium as on the Note 60 Ultra, and mostly on par with the one on the previous Note 50 Pro 4G. There is a 50MP OIS primary, an 8MP ultrawide, and a 13MP selfie camera.

The Note 60 Pro packs JBL-tuned stereo speakers, Wi-Fi 6 connectivity, FM radio. It has a large battery with 6,500mAh capacity and support for 90W wired, 30W wireless, plus reverse wired and wireless charging. Bypass charging is also available.

The phone boots Android 16 with XOS 16 and it's eligible for three major Android updates.

And the cute active matrix display on the back gives it some extra character.

Here's the complete specs sheet before we get this review started.

Infinix Note 60 Pro specs at a glance:

  • Body: 162.4x77.2x7.4mm, 202g; Glass front (Gorilla Glass 7i), aluminum frame, glass back; IP64 dust tight and water resistant (water splashes), RGB notification light & active matrix display (on the back).
  • Display: 6.78" AMOLED, 144Hz, 2304Hz PWM, 700 nits (typ), 1600 nits (HBM), 4500 nits (peak), 1208x2644px resolution, 19.7:9 aspect ratio, 429ppi; Ultra HDR support.
  • Chipset: Qualcomm SM7635-AC Snapdragon 7s Gen 4 (4 nm).
  • Memory: 128GB 8GB RAM, 256GB 8GB RAM, 256GB 12GB RAM; UFS 2.2.
  • OS/Software: Android 16, up to 3 major Android upgrades, XOS 16.
  • Rear camera: Wide (main): 50 MP, f/1.6, 0.8µm, PDAF, OIS; Ultra wide angle: 8 MP, f/2.2, 112˚, 1.12µm.
  • Front camera: 13 MP, f/2.2, (wide), 1.12µm.
  • Video capture: Rear camera: 4K@30fps, 1080p@30/60/120fps; Front camera: 4K@30fps, 1080p@30/60fps.
  • Battery: 6500mAh; 90W wired, 50% in 16 min, 100% in 41 min (6500 mAh), 90W wired, 50% in 14 min, 100% in 35 min (6000 mAh), 30W wireless, 7.5W reverse wired, 5W reverse wireless, Bypass Charging (gaming, navigating).
  • Connectivity: 5G; eSIM; Wi-Fi 6; BT 5.4; NFC; FM radio; Infrared port.
  • Misc: Fingerprint reader (under display, optical); stereo speakers; Health Monitor sensor in the frame.

The Infinix Note 60 Pro has IP64 protection that offers basic splash resistance, whereas other mid-rangers are offering IP68, IP69 and even IP69K protection levels. And that's our only complaint at specs sheet level.

Unboxing the Infinix Note 60 Pro

The Infinix Note 60 Pro ships inside a silver-colored retail box that's full of goodies. Inside you will find a 90W charger, a USB cable (A-C), and a MagSafe-enabled case.

That's not all. The retail bundle also contains an EarPods-like headset. And let's not forget the protective folio atop the screen.
