The Oppo K15 has been quietly unveiled in China, joining the K15 Pro and K15 Pro+, which debuted in April. The standard K15 retains the same design language as its Pro siblings and also features a built-in active cooling fan.

It is already available for purchase in China and is being offered in a sole 12GB/256GB variant, which is priced at CNY 2,299 ($340).

The handset sports a 6.59-inch AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate, 1,200 nits peak brightness, and 1.5K resolution. It also comes with an in-display fingerprint scanner.

At the back, the Oppo K15 features a dual-rear camera setup with a 50MP main sensor with OIS and a 50MP ultrawide unit. For selfies, the device offers a 50MP front camera.

It is powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 7360 Super chipset, which is paired with LPDDR4X RAM and UFS 3.1 storage. The in-built active cooling fan keeps the device running cool under load.

The phone packs an 8,000mAh battery with support for 80W wired fast charging. It gets an IP69 rating and weighs 205 grams.

Connectivity features include 5G, dual 4G, dual-band Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.4, GPS, NFC, and an Infrared sensor. In terms of software, the Oppo K15 runs Android 16-based ColorOS 16.

It is offered in Gale Grey and Speed White color options and is already on sale in China.
