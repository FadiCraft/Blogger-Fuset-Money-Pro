With Samsung's Galaxy Unpacked event less than a day away, leaks continue to pour in. The latest one gives us another look at the upcoming wide-screen Galaxy Z Fold8 ahead of its expected debut.

Tipster Roland Quandt has shared a new set of official-looking Galaxy Z Fold8 (wide) renders on BlueSky, offering another look at Samsung's upcoming foldable.

The renders showcase the Galaxy Z Fold8 Wide in three color options. In line with recent leaks, the smartphone appears in Cream, Graphite, and Lavender finishes.

A Pistachio variant is also rumored to be available exclusively through Samsung's online store.

The second image highlights the foldable's cover display, which is rumored to measure 5.5-inches and feature a 16:10 aspect ratio with slim, uniform bezels.

It also offers a look at the bottom edge, revealing the speaker grille, USB Type-C port, and microphone cutouts.

Another render focuses on the Galaxy Z Fold8 Wide's Flex Titanium hinge layers used for the inner 7.6-inch display that is said to offer a 4:3 aspect ratio.

Samsung recently detailed its Flex Titanium technology, which will debut with its upcoming foldables. The company says it combines a titanium alloy film with a flexible titanium plate to improve durability, reduce display creasing, and enhance the overall viewing experience.

The Galaxy Z Fold8 will be powered by the Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 for Galaxy chipset and is rumored to pack a 4,800mAh battery. It is said to offer a 50MP main rear camera alongside another 50MP ultrawide unit.
